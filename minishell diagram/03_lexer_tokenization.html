✂️ Lexer: Chopping Input Into Tokens

The first real step — turning a raw string into meaningful pieces

What is "Lexing" (Tokenization)?

Imagine someone says: "I want three large pizzas". Your brain automatically breaks this into meaningful units: [I] [want] [three] [large] [pizzas]. That's exactly what a lexer does to shell input.

The lexer takes a raw string and splits it into tokens — labeled chunks that the parser can understand.

📬 Mail Sorting Analogy: A post office receives a pile of letters (raw string). The first step is sorting: this is a letter, this is a package, this is a postcard (different token types). You don't deliver anything yet — you just categorize. That's lexing.

The Tricky Part: Quotes!

Quotes change how the lexer works. This is where most bugs happen in minishell:

⚠ Common 42 Mistake: Treating quotes as token delimiters. They're NOT. Quotes are part of the WORD token. echo "hello"'world'end is ONE token: helloworld end. The quotes just control how characters inside are interpreted.
