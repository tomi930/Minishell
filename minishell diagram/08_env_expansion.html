🔤 Environment Variables & Expansion

How $HOME becomes /home/tomi and why $? tracks exit codes

What Are Environment Variables?

Env vars are key=value pairs that every process inherits from its parent. They carry configuration info: where your home is, what language to use, where to find programs.

🧬 DNA Analogy: Environment variables are like DNA. When a parent process creates a child (fork), the child inherits a COPY of all env vars — just like biological inheritance. The child can modify its copy without affecting the parent. export is like adding a new gene. unset is like removing one.

Variable Expansion: When $ Becomes a Value

Expansion happens BEFORE execution. The command never sees the $ — it receives the expanded value.
