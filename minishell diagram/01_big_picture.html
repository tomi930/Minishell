🐚 What Is a Shell?

Understanding the big picture before writing a single line of code

The Shell = The Middleman

A shell is just a program that sits between YOU and the operating system. When you type a command, the shell figures out what you want, finds the right program, and asks the OS to run it.

🍔 Restaurant Analogy: Think of a restaurant. You (the user) tell the waiter (the shell) what you want. The waiter translates your order, sends it to the kitchen (the OS/kernel), and brings back the result (output). The waiter doesn't cook — it just manages communication.

Why "Mini" Shell?

Real bash has ~70,000 lines of code and handles scripting, arrays, functions, arithmetic, job control, and much more. Your minishell focuses on the core mechanics — the fundamental concepts that make a shell work. Once you understand these, you understand how ALL shells work under the hood.

🏗️ Building Analogy: Bash is a skyscraper. Minishell is building a solid 2-story house. Same foundation (fork, exec, pipes, redirections), same engineering principles. If you can build the house, you understand how the skyscraper stands.
